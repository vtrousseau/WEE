Holistic Approach to WEE:
REE-CALL Programme in Bangladesh

Oxfam’s Resilience through Economic Empowerment, Climate Adaptation, Leadership and Learning (REE-CALL) programme in Bangladesh aims to build knowledge and capacity within communities to anticipate and cope with climate and disaster-related challenges. It promotes gender justice, good governance, access to services and economic empowerment as foundations for building improved disaster preparedness and resilience.

In a series of short videos, M. B. Akhter, Programme Director, presents REE-CALL’s holistic approach to women’s economic empowerment.

Holistic approach to addressing barriers faced by communities

REE-CALL's holistic approach is based on a set of functional/thematic community-based organisations (CBO). CBOs involve all community members and tackle economic, cultural and social issues with a particular emphasis on women.

Designing a community action plan

After identifying areas particularly vulnarable to climate change and disaster, community members jointly design a community action plan.

Thematic focus

In the videos below, M. B. Akhter explains in more detail how REE-CALL addresses unpaid care work, and how distinct community-based organisations contribute to increasing women's social captial, to create economic opportunities and to contribute to broader systemic change.

Bringing unpaid care to the fore

Building on evidence at community level, the programme triggered change at household level and worked with private companies and the State to address unpaid care.

Increasing social capital

Women traditionally have limited spaces to sit and talk. The Adda, a forum where women can exchange, creates solidarity among women in the community and triggers social norms change.

Creating employment opportunities and systemic change

CBOs are connecting land owners with the landless, and linking communities with the local and central governments to advocate for adequate infrastructure and services for poor rural communities.

Transforming lives and systems

REE-CALL's programmatic approach aims to bring change at individual, household, community and national level. The videos below focus on how transformational change happens and the trajectory from individual to systemic change.

Collective action: Bringing change from local to national level

While CBOs aim to address issues locally, REE-CALL ambitions to trigger change at district and national level.

Benefiting an entire community

CBO committees are limited to a limited number of individuals. Nevertheless, they benefit the entire community as illustrated by the employment, WASH and violence against women CBOs.

Transformational changes

Women's leadership brings about transformation of formal and informal power structures, and shifting social norms to support women's empowerment at political, social and economic level.

Economic changes

Women's participation in markets has greatly increased, as well as both family and women's income.
